## The Story Behind This

This one started as a random thought. One of those ideas that hits you out of nowhere and won't leave — a detective, a small Nebraska town, a missing kid, a conspiracy held together by twenty years of people choosing not to look.

I wanted to make it a pixel game. The kind with a dark overworld, flickering lampposts, doors you can walk into, suspects with portraits that shift when they're lying. That version still lives in my head.

But I didn't want to let the idea die waiting for the right format. So I built it as a text RPG instead — branching choices, a live stat system, four endings, procedural music that gets darker as the chapters go. A cork board detective theme because if you're going to tell a story about evidence and secrets, it should feel like a room covered in red string.

Maybe someday it becomes the pixel game. For now, this is the version that exists. I'd rather give an idea a day of light than let it disappear forever.

---

## The Case

You are **Detective Ethan Mercer**. Your wife was buried this morning. Your son has been missing for five years. And your phone just rang — another body, out at the old mill.

Hollow Creek, Nebraska. Population: 4,102. A church, a diner, a precinct, and a chief of police who has spent twenty years knowing exactly when to look away.

Someone just stopped letting him.

Six chapters. Every choice shifts your stress, your guilt, your hope, how much truth survives to the end. The people you trust and the evidence you protect will determine which of the four endings you reach.

None of them are clean.

## How to Play

Open the HTML file in any browser. Turn the music on before you start — it builds with the story.

No installs. No accounts. Just a file and a town full of people keeping secrets.
